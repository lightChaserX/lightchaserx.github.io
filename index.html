Zhixiang Wang | 汪智祥

I am a MEXT-funded PhD student at the University of Tokyo with Shin'ichi Satoh starting in Oct. 2021. Our lab is affiliated with National Institute of Informatics. Part of my research was supported by RIISE. Prior to this, I completed my Master's degree at National Taiwan University with Yung-Yu Chuang.

My research interest focuses on computational imaging and computational photography. Throughout my studies, I have been fortunate to collaborate closely with Yinqiang Zheng, Jia-Bin Huang, Jian Wang, and Jinwei Gu. In 2022, I interned with Shree Nayar's team at Snap Research. During my master's study, I was fortunate to work at National Institute of Informatics as a research intern. I also spent some time at Nanyang Technological University in Singapore.

Research

My research is centered on Computational Photography and Imaging, with a focus on capturing the intricacies of the physical world through innovative hardware and algorithmic designs. I am now keen on blending physical principles with generative models for reconstructions and interactions of the physical world.
